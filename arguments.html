Arguments Against the Idea

The theory of Continental Drift was not widely accepted and there were two main arguemnts for this

1.) What mechanism could move the continents

In the early 1900's our technology was nowhere near what we have now. When thinking about something as big a continent moving, it's hard to wrap your head around it. Even if Wegener was making good points, this doesn't change the fact that the people didn't know how the continents were moving which caused doubt within the community.

2.) Validity of the Evidence

Wegener had 5 main pieces of evidence that supported his thesis of continental motion. 1.) Jigsaw puzzle fit, 2.) same geological formations in different continents, 3.) fossil species in different continents, 4.) location of glacial morained indicated that continentws had different relations to the poles compared to now, 5.) Geodetic difference between Greenland and Europe was increasing.

Other geologists argued that the formation of the same rock does not have to be in the same location. There could have been a coincidence that the rocks matched. Along with this the idea of land bridges combatted his argument of fossils in different locations. As for the geodetic evidence, they believed it was just an error in calculation.

The reasons and explanations above are the reason that the idea of continental drift was not accepted until later on when more evidence would be presented.
